# Dialpad — Brand Voice Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines the default Dialpad voice for official writing. The voice stays consistent across channels; the tone adapts to the situation and audience.

---

## Core Voice

| Principle | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| **Helpful, not handholding** | Make information easy to follow, explain benefits clearly | Talk down to the reader |
| **Real, not too tough** | Use business-casual language, be plain-spoken, honest, direct, and empathetic | Be stiff, cold, or overly formal |
| **Welcoming, not overly familiar** | Sound warm and approachable, use jargon only when necessary | Use sarcasm or forced friendliness |
| **Bold, not arrogant** | Be confident, explain the why and how | Sound pushy or self-important |

---

## Tone by Context

### Everyday product copy
Keep it simple, unobtrusive, and consistent so people can get work done quickly. Don't draw attention to the interface — get out of the way.

### Education and guidance
Help people feel confident. Explain why an action matters, and break complex tasks into manageable steps.

### Errors
Clearly explain what happened and how to fix it. Avoid dramatic or scary wording for simple issues.

### Serious problems
Explain impact clearly, show empathy when appropriate, and provide concrete next steps. Don't minimize or deflect.

### Announcements and launches
Focus on what changed, what it does, and what the audience should expect. Do not assume people are excited just because something is new.

---

## Writing Style Rules

- **Write for the reader first:** define the audience, the purpose, and the action they should take after reading.
- **Use positive, constructive phrasing** that focuses on what can be done or improved.
- **Sound human and conversational** — not slangy, robotic, or pedantic.
- **Be active and direct.** Get to the point fast, lead with the main idea, and cut unnecessary words.
- **Prefer short, scannable writing** — most readers skim.

---

## Inclusive and Global Writing

- Avoid ableist, gendered, violent, divisive, or insulting language.
- Avoid words like *simply*, *easy*, *quickly*, or similar phrasing in procedural guidance.
- Write for a global audience: use short sentences, present tense, simple words, and avoid idioms, slang, culture-specific references, and unnecessary humor.
- Keep content accessible: make headings clear, label things explicitly, and do not rely on visuals alone to convey meaning.

---

## Brand Terminology

| Prefer | Avoid |
|---|---|
| customers, people, employees, or *you* | *users* (in official external writing) |
| contact center platform | call center platform |
| unified communications platform | business phone system (unless SEO or use case requires the narrower term) |

## Pre-Publish Checklist

Before publishing, confirm the draft is:

- [ ] Clear
- [ ] Concise
- [ ] Human
- [ ] Confident
- [ ] Reader-centered
- [ ] Inclusive
- [ ] Accurate without overpromising
- [ ] Aligned with approved Dialpad terminology
