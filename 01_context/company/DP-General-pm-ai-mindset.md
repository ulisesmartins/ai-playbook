# Product Mindset — Why AI Matters for Product Management (Right Now)

## Purpose

This file captures Dialpad's PM leadership direction on AI adoption. Load it when generating
any PM-facing output — PRDs, task breakdowns, coaching prompts, or onboarding materials —
to ground the tone and expectations in the actual operating standard.

---

## Why AI Matters Right Now

AI is the most powerful leverage tool we've had in years for accelerating the parts of our
work that usually slow us down:

- **Speed to clarity** — Turn messy inputs into crisp problem statements, hypotheses, and
  PRDs in hours, not days.

- **Faster iteration** — Build your own prototypes, generate multiple options for flows,
  messaging, edge cases, and acceptance criteria so we explore more before we commit.

- **Better quality** — Catch gaps earlier: risks, dependencies, non-obvious edge cases, and
  "gotchas" that show up late if we don't pressure-test.

- **Unblocking** — Draft the first version of anything (doc, spec, announcement, experiment
  plan) so you're never stuck staring at a blank page.

- **Sharper communication** — Translate between audiences — engineering, design, execs,
  support — without losing intent.

## What "AI Daily" Looks Like (Practical, Not Theoretical)

Every PM uses AI as a default companion in their workflow — every day. There is nothing you
do on a given day where the question **"Can AI help with this?"** shouldn't be asked.
Ask it over and over again.

### 1. Kickstart work (minutes, not hours)

- "Help me write a one-page PRD for X with goals, non-goals, risks, and metrics."
- "Give me 3 alternative approaches and their tradeoffs."
- "List edge cases and failure modes for this flow."

### 2. Pressure-test decisions

- "Argue against this roadmap choice — what would I be missing?"
- "What assumptions are embedded here? Which ones are riskiest?"
- "What's the simplest experiment to validate this?"

### 3. Unblock cross-functional execution

- "Turn these notes into a JIRA-ready breakdown with dependencies."
- "Draft a design review agenda and the questions we must answer."
- "Write the launch plan: beta criteria, comms, enablement, success metrics."

### 4. Make customers louder

- "Summarize these call notes into themes and top pain points."
- "Draft a customer-facing explanation of this change in plain language."
- "Generate interview questions to validate the top 2 hypotheses."

## The Expectation

Every PM at Dialpad is expected to:

1. **Use AI daily** for drafting, analysis, brainstorming, and unblocking.
2. **Bring AI outputs into your work** — not as final answers, but as accelerators.
3. **Share what works** — prompts, workflows, and "this saved me 3 hours" moments with the team.

If you're thinking _"I already do"_ — help others level up.
If you're thinking _"I'm not sure where to start"_ — start with the same question for
everything you do: **"Can AI help with that?"** and apply the CRIT framework.

## The Mindset Shift

We are not here to produce words — we are here to create outcomes. The faster we can get
to the best version of the truth — what customers need, what we can build, what will move
the needle — the more impact we will have.

AI helps us move:

| From | To |
|---|---|
| Busywork | Leverage |
| Waiting | Momentum |
| Good ideas | Shipped value |

---

## How This Applies in Agent Sessions

When Claude Code is helping a PM, it should reflect this mindset in every output:

- **Never produce words for the sake of words.** Every artifact should move the team toward a decision or unblock execution.
- **Default to drafts, not questions.** Give the PM something to react to — a draft PRD, a gap list, a set of options — not a questionnaire.
- **Accelerate, don't replace judgment.** Flag risks, surface assumptions, offer alternatives — but leave the call to the PM.
- **Push for specificity.** If the PM's input is vague, the output should name what's missing and offer the most reasonable interpretation — not produce vague output in return.
- **Apply the CRIT framework** when pressure-testing decisions: **C**hallenges (what's hardest), **R**isks (what could go wrong), **I**mplications (what follows from this), **T**radeoffs (what we give up).
